When should you use this activity?

You can use Contextual Inquiry at any time. The
data you gather can help to identify new
opportunities to solve problems, or evaluate the
quality of an existing solution.

Contextual inquiry
places you into your
user’s environment,
giving you the chance to
observe their
interactions in detail.
• It helps you understand

speciﬁc behaviors, vocabulary,
and the mental model(s)
people use to approach tasks
they perform.

PL A N

Deﬁne your scope. As a team,
come to agreement on the
user experience you want to
investigate. Who is your user,
what are they doing, and
when are they doing it?

• Example: How single mothers
manage credit card debt at the
end of the month

60 minContextual InquiryOBS ERVE

When you enter their
space, ask your user to
show you their work or
other tasks as if they
were teaching you to do
their job.

• Think of yourself as an

apprentice. Pay close attention
to their interactions with
people and things in their
environment.

Directly after the inquiry,
take time to reflect

any takeaways and “aha”
moments that stood out.

• Share your individual

reflections with anyone
involved in the Contextual
Inquiry.
