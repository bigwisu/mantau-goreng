# IBM Enterprise Design Thinking Framework

Source: https://www.ibm.com/training/enterprise-design-thinking/framework

Design thinking re-envisioned for the modern enterprise.

## Overview

Enterprise Design Thinking® is a framework to solve users' problems at the speed and scale of the modern enterprise. It's built on the belief that the systems of the world should work in service of people.

## The Principles

The foundation for delivering solutions that meet or exceed user expectations:

### 1. A Focus on User Outcomes
**See problems and solutions as an ongoing conversation.**

Drive business by helping users achieve their goals. Success is measured by how well we help users reach their objectives, not just by shipping features.

### 2. Restless Reinvention
**Stay essential by treating everything as a prototype.**

Continuously improve and adapt. Nothing is ever "done" - every solution is an opportunity to learn and evolve based on user feedback and changing needs.

### 3. Diverse Empowered Teams
**Move faster by empowering diverse teams to act.**

Bring together people with different perspectives, skills, and backgrounds. Give them the autonomy to make decisions and take action without excessive bureaucracy.

## The Loop

A continuous cycle of observing, reflecting, and making that drives us to understand the present and envision the future.

### Observe
**Immerse yourself in the real world.**

Get out of the building and into users' environments. Watch how they work, understand their context, and identify their pain points through direct observation.

### Reflect
**Come together and look within.**

Bring the team together to synthesize what you've learned. Identify patterns, challenge assumptions, and align on what matters most to users.

### Make
**Give concrete form to abstract ideas.**

Build prototypes, create mockups, write code - make your ideas tangible so you can test them with real users and learn from the results.

### Finding Your Momentum

1. **Start by reflecting** - What are your capabilities? What problem are you solving? What do you know and not know?
2. **Create a plan** - Decide whether to observe users or make prototypes first
3. **Do all three** - Observing and reflecting without making is analysis paralysis. Making and reflecting without observing is blind faith.
4. **Keep looping** - Take as many loops as time allows, but be ready to commit to decisions
5. **Stay moving** - Even after you commit, you'll discover new problems and ideas

## The Keys

Three techniques for keeping diverse teams aligned on what matters to users:

### Hills
**Statements of intent written as meaningful user outcomes.**

Hills tell you where to go, not how to get there. They empower teams to explore breakthrough ideas without losing sight of the goal.

**Format:** Who (user) + What (capability) + Wow (differentiating value)

Example: "A developer can deploy a new microservice in under 5 minutes, making experimentation effortless."

### Playbacks
**Storytelling sessions to share work and exchange feedback.**

Playbacks bring stakeholders into the Loop in a safe space. They reveal misalignment and measure progress against the big picture problem you're solving.

**Purpose:**
- Share progress with stakeholders
- Get feedback early and often
- Identify misalignment before it becomes costly
- Keep everyone focused on user outcomes

### Sponsor Users
**Real-world users who regularly contribute their domain expertise.**

Sponsor users help you stay in touch with real users' real-world needs throughout the project. They're not just interviewed once - they're ongoing partners in the design process.

**Benefits:**
- Continuous reality check on your assumptions
- Direct access to domain expertise
- Early warning system for misaligned solutions
- Built-in advocates when you launch
