# IBM Enterprise Design Thinking

## Overview

IBM Enterprise Design Thinking® is a framework for solving users' problems at the speed and scale of the modern enterprise. It's built on the belief that the systems of the world should work in service of people.

This skill provides guidance on applying IBM's Enterprise Design Thinking framework, including:
- **The Principles**: User Outcomes, Restless Reinvention, Diverse Empowered Teams
- **The Loop**: Observe, Reflect, Make (continuous cycle)
- **The Keys**: Hills, Playbacks, Sponsor Users (alignment techniques)
- **The Toolkit**: 29 guided activities for research, planning, ideation, testing, and team alignment

**Core Philosophy**: Design thinking re-envisioned for the modern enterprise - moving from theory to practice at scale.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when:

- **Defining AI strategy**: Apply AI Essentials activities (Intent, Data, Understanding, Reasoning, Knowledge)

## The Three Principles

### 1. A Focus on User Outcomes
**See problems and solutions as an ongoing conversation.**

Drive business by helping users achieve their goals. Success is measured by how well we help users reach their objectives, not just by shipping features.

**In Practice:**
- Start every initiative by defining user outcomes, not features
- Measure success by user goal achievement
- Treat every solution as part of an ongoing conversation with users

### 2. Restless Reinvention
**Stay essential by treating everything as a prototype.**

Continuously improve and adapt. Nothing is ever "done" - every solution is an opportunity to learn and evolve based on user feedback and changing needs.

**In Practice:**
- Ship early and iterate based on feedback
- Challenge assumptions regularly
- Embrace change as opportunity for improvement

### 3. Diverse Empowered Teams
**Move faster by empowering diverse teams to act.**

Bring together people with different perspectives, skills, and backgrounds. Give them the autonomy to make decisions and take action without excessive bureaucracy.

**In Practice:**
- Build cross-functional teams with diverse perspectives
- Empower teams to make decisions without excessive approval chains
- Create psychological safety for experimentation

## The Loop: Observe → Reflect → Make

A continuous cycle that drives understanding of the present and envisions the future.

### Observe
**Immerse yourself in the real world.**

Get out of the building and into users' environments. Watch how they work, understand their context, and identify their pain points through direct observation.

**Activities:**
- Contextual Inquiry (Toolkit #25)
- Interviews (Toolkit #26)
- Direct observation in user environments

### Reflect
**Come together and look within.**

Bring the team together to synthesize what you've learned. Identify patterns, challenge assumptions, and align on what matters most to users.

**Activities:**
- Empathy Map (Toolkit #8)
- Needs Statements (Toolkit #9)
- Assumptions and Questions (Toolkit #11)

### Make
**Give concrete form to abstract ideas.**

Build prototypes, create mockups, write code - make your ideas tangible so you can test them with real users and learn from the results.

**Activities:**
- Paper Prototypes (Toolkit #23)
- Wireframes (Toolkit #24)
- Storyboards (Toolkit #19)

### Finding Your Momentum

1. **Start by reflecting** - What are your capabilities? What problem are you solving? What do you know and not know?
2. **Create a plan** - Decide whether to observe users or make prototypes first
3. **Do all three** - Observing and reflecting without making is analysis paralysis. Making and reflecting without observing is blind faith.
4. **Keep looping** - Take as many loops as time allows, but be ready to commit to decisions
5. **Stay moving** - Even after you commit, you'll discover new problems and ideas

## The Keys: Alignment Techniques

Three techniques for keeping diverse teams aligned on what matters to users.

### Hills
**Statements of intent written as meaningful user outcomes.**

Hills tell you where to go, not how to get there. They empower teams to explore breakthrough ideas without losing sight of the goal.

**Format:** Who (user) + What (capability) + Wow (differentiating value)

**Example:** "A developer can deploy a new microservice in under 5 minutes, making experimentation effortless."

**Activity:** Hills (Toolkit #13)

### Playbacks
**Storytelling sessions to share work and exchange feedback.**

Playbacks bring stakeholders into the Loop in a safe space. They reveal misalignment and measure progress against the big picture problem you're solving.

**Purpose:**
- Share progress with stakeholders
- Get feedback early and often
- Identify misalignment before it becomes costly
- Keep everyone focused on user outcomes

**Activity:** Playbacks (Toolkit #29)

### Sponsor Users
**Real-world users who regularly contribute their domain expertise.**

Sponsor users help you stay in touch with real users' real-world needs throughout the project. They're not just interviewed once - they're ongoing partners in the design process.

**Benefits:**
- Continuous reality check on your assumptions
- Direct access to domain expertise
- Early warning system for misaligned solutions
- Built-in advocates when you launch

## Workflow Guidance

### Starting a New Project

1. **Reflect on capabilities and constraints** - What do you know? What don't you know?
2. **Define Hills** - Write user outcome statements (Who + What + Wow)
3. **Identify Sponsor Users** - Find real users to partner with throughout
4. **Plan your first Loop** - Decide whether to Observe or Make first
5. **Schedule Playbacks** - Set regular storytelling sessions with stakeholders

### During Active Development

1. **Keep Looping** - Continuously Observe, Reflect, Make
2. **Hold regular Playbacks** - Share progress and get feedback
3. **Engage Sponsor Users** - Check assumptions against real-world needs
4. **Run Retrospectives** - Improve team processes regularly
5. **Revisit Hills** - Ensure you're still aligned on user outcomes

### When Facing Challenges

- **Misalignment?** → Run a Playback to surface issues
- **Unclear direction?** → Revisit or rewrite your Hills
- **Too many ideas?** → Use Prioritization Grid
- **Assumptions unchecked?** → Run Assumptions and Questions activity
- **Lost touch with users?** → Engage Sponsor Users or conduct Contextual Inquiry

## Key Principles for Facilitators

1. **Focus on user outcomes, not features** - Always start with "What does the user need to achieve?"
2. **Embrace diverse perspectives** - The best solutions come from cross-functional collaboration
3. **Make ideas tangible quickly** - Don't overthink, prototype and test
4. **Iterate relentlessly** - Nothing is ever "done", everything is a prototype
5. **Stay aligned through storytelling** - Use Playbacks to keep everyone on the same page
6. **Partner with real users** - Sponsor Users keep you grounded in reality
