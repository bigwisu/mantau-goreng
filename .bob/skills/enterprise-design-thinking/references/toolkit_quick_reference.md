# IBM Enterprise Design Thinking Toolkit - Quick Reference

This quick reference provides an overview of all 29 toolkit activities organized by category. For detailed instructions, see the full documentation in `docs/IBM/`.

## Activity Selection Guide

### When to Use Each Activity

| Situation | Recommended Activities |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Starting a new project | Hills (#13), Stakeholder Map (#10), Hopes and Fears (#28) |
| Need to understand users | Contextual Inquiry (#25), Interviews (#26), Empathy Map (#8) |
| Planning next steps | Experience-based Roadmap (#12), Prioritization Grid (#14), Research Plan (#15) |
| Generating ideas | Big Idea Vignettes (#17), Storyboards (#19), To-be Scenario Map (#20) |
| Testing concepts | Paper Prototypes (#23), Cognitive Walkthrough (#21), Feedback Grid (#22) |
| Team alignment issues | Playbacks (#29), Daily Syncs (#27), Retrospectives (#30) |
| Defining AI strategy | AI Essentials series (#2-6) |
| Reality check needed | Assumptions and Questions (#11) |

## Define Your AI Strategy (5 activities)

### #2: AI Essentials: Intent
**Purpose:** Determine why you would or wouldn't use AI to solve your users' problems  
**Duration:** ~60 min  
**When:** Early in AI project planning  
**Output:** Clear rationale for AI use or non-use

### #3: AI Essentials: Data
**Purpose:** Identify the data that you have and what data you need to get  
**Duration:** ~60 min  
**When:** After establishing AI intent  
**Output:** Data inventory and acquisition plan

### #4: AI Essentials: Understanding
**Purpose:** Deconstruct your data sources into components needed to teach your AI  
**Duration:** ~60 min  
**When:** After data identification  
**Output:** Data component breakdown

### #5: AI Essentials: Reasoning
**Purpose:** Revisit your big ideas and bring them down to earth  
**Duration:** ~60 min  
**When:** Mid-project reality check  
**Output:** Grounded, feasible AI concepts

### #6: AI Essentials: Knowledge
**Purpose:** Brainstorm the impact that your AI can have on people and the world  
**Duration:** ~60 min  
**When:** Before implementation  
**Output:** Impact assessment and ethical considerations

## Synthesize Research (4 activities)

### #7: As-is Scenario Map
**Purpose:** Build a better understanding of your users' current experience  
**Duration:** ~90 min  
**When:** After initial user research  
**Output:** Visual map of current user journey

### #8: Empathy Map
**Purpose:** Build empathy for your users through a conversation informed by your team's observations  
**Duration:** ~30 min  
**When:** After observation sessions or before important decisions  
**Output:** Shared understanding of user thoughts, feelings, actions

### #9: Needs Statements
**Purpose:** Outline what users need in order to achieve their goals  
**Duration:** ~60 min  
**When:** After empathy mapping or user research  
**Output:** Clear user need statements

### #10: Stakeholder Map
**Purpose:** Identify project stakeholders, their expectations and their relationship to each other  
**Duration:** ~45 min  
**When:** Project kickoff or when alignment issues arise  
**Output:** Visual stakeholder relationship map

---

## Plan Your Work (6 activities)

### #11: Assumptions and Questions
**Purpose:** When your team needs a "reality check," identify your assumptions and the best ways to address them  
**Duration:** ~60 min  
**When:** Before major decisions or when feeling uncertain  
**Output:** Documented assumptions and validation plan

### #12: Experience-based Roadmap
**Purpose:** Break down your long-term experience into the most essential near-term outcomes for your user  
**Duration:** ~90 min  
**When:** After defining Hills, before sprint planning  
**Output:** Phased roadmap focused on user experience

### #13: Hills
**Purpose:** Use Hills to clearly state your intent in terms of user and market value  
**Duration:** ~60 min  
**When:** Beginning of project or initiative  
**Output:** 1-3 Hill statements (Who + What + Wow)

### #14: Prioritization Grid
**Purpose:** Decide your next move by focusing on the intersection of importance and feasibility  
**Duration:** ~45 min  
**When:** When you have multiple options to evaluate  
**Output:** Prioritized list of initiatives

### #15: Research Plan
**Purpose:** Define your goals and action steps to understand your users and address your team's unknowns  
**Duration:** ~60 min  
**When:** Before conducting user research  
**Output:** Structured research plan with methods and timeline

### #16: User Stories
**Purpose:** Describe requirements as discrete tasks a user must do to reach their goal  
**Duration:** ~60 min  
**When:** During sprint planning or backlog refinement  
**Output:** User story cards in "As a... I want... So that..." format

## Generate New Ideas (4 activities)

### #17: Big Idea Vignettes
**Purpose:** Rapidly diverge on a breadth of possible solutions to meet your users' needs  
**Duration:** ~90 min  
**When:** Early ideation phase  
**Output:** Multiple diverse solution concepts

### #18: Speculative Design Stories
**Purpose:** Create short stories that predict your users' future problems and produce novel solutions to address those problems  
**Duration:** ~120 min  
**When:** Exploring future scenarios or disruptive innovation  
**Output:** Future-focused narrative scenarios

### #19: Storyboards
**Purpose:** Communicate ideas through visual stories that showcase how they fit into your users' lives  
**Duration:** ~60 min  
**When:** After initial ideation, before prototyping  
**Output:** Sequential visual narrative of user experience

### #20: To-be Scenario Map
**Purpose:** Draft a vision of your user's future experience to show how your ideas address their current needs  
**Duration:** ~90 min  
**When:** After ideation, to visualize future state  
**Output:** Visual map of desired future user journey

---

## Test Your Ideas (4 activities)

### #21: Cognitive Walkthrough
**Purpose:** Observe users interacting with a prototype in order to evaluate its success  
**Duration:** ~60 min per session  
**When:** After creating prototype, before full development  
**Output:** Usability insights and improvement opportunities

### #22: Feedback Grid
**Purpose:** Gather and organize feedback from users, team members or stakeholders  
**Duration:** ~30 min  
**When:** After presentations, demos, or testing sessions  
**Output:** Organized feedback in categories (likes, wishes, questions, ideas)

### #23: Paper Prototypes
**Purpose:** Communicate your ideas quickly by using cheap and versatile materials  
**Duration:** ~60 min  
**When:** Early concept testing, before digital prototypes  
**Output:** Low-fidelity physical prototypes

### #24: Wireframes
**Purpose:** Prototype digital user interfaces at low and medium fidelity to quickly get feedback  
**Duration:** ~90 min  
**When:** After paper prototypes, before high-fidelity design  
**Output:** Digital interface mockups

---

## Gather User Data (2 activities)

### #25: Contextual Inquiry
**Purpose:** Observe users, in their daily context, to understand the "how" and "why" behind their behavior  
**Duration:** ~90 min per session  
**When:** Early research phase, in user's environment  
**Output:** Rich contextual observations and insights

### #26: Interviews
**Purpose:** Get to know users or stakeholders by reaching out and talking to them  
**Duration:** ~60 min per interview  
**When:** Throughout project for various research needs  
**Output:** Interview notes and key insights

---

## Align Your Team (4 activities)

### #27: Daily Syncs
**Purpose:** Stay informed on the team's actions, ask for assistance, and surface blockers early  
**Duration:** ~15 min daily  
**When:** Every working day  
**Output:** Shared awareness and unblocked progress

### #28: Hopes and Fears
**Purpose:** Learn and discuss each others' hopes and fears before starting a project or onboarding new team members  
**Duration:** ~45 min  
**When:** Project kickoff or team changes  
**Output:** Psychological safety and shared expectations

### #29: Playbacks
**Purpose:** Tell stories to share your work and exchange feedback  
**Duration:** ~60-90 min  
**When:** Regular intervals (weekly/bi-weekly) throughout project  
**Output:** Stakeholder alignment and actionable feedback

### #30: Retrospectives
**Purpose:** Reflect together at the end of an iteration and find ways to improve the way you work together  
**Duration:** ~60 min  
**When:** End of sprint/iteration or project phase  
**Output:** Team improvements and action items

## Activity Combinations

### Common Workflows

**Starting a New Project:**
1. Hopes and Fears (#28) - Build team trust
2. Stakeholder Map (#10) - Identify key players
3. Hills (#13) - Define user outcomes
4. Research Plan (#15) - Plan user research

**Understanding Users:**
1. Contextual Inquiry (#25) or Interviews (#26) - Gather data
2. Empathy Map (#8) - Synthesize observations
3. Needs Statements (#9) - Articulate user needs
4. As-is Scenario Map (#7) - Map current experience

**Planning Solutions:**
1. Assumptions and Questions (#11) - Reality check
2. Big Idea Vignettes (#17) - Generate ideas
3. Prioritization Grid (#14) - Select best ideas
4. Experience-based Roadmap (#12) - Plan delivery

**Testing and Iterating:**
1. Paper Prototypes (#23) - Quick concepts
2. Cognitive Walkthrough (#21) - Test with users
3. Feedback Grid (#22) - Organize feedback
4. Wireframes (#24) - Refine design

**Maintaining Alignment:**
1. Daily Syncs (#27) - Daily coordination
2. Playbacks (#29) - Regular stakeholder updates
3. Retrospectives (#30) - Continuous improvement

## Tips for Success

1. **Don't skip Observe** - Even if you think you know your users, observe them in context
2. **Make it tangible** - Abstract discussions don't drive decisions; prototypes do
3. **Playback regularly** - Don't wait until the end to share progress
4. **Engage Sponsor Users** - Real users throughout the process, not just at the beginning
5. **Keep looping** - Observe → Reflect → Make is continuous, not linear
6. **Write Hills early** - Clear user outcomes prevent scope creep
7. **Embrace diverse perspectives** - The best solutions come from cross-functional teams
8. **Treat everything as a prototype** - Nothing is ever "done"
